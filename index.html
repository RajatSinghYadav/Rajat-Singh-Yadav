About

Economist working across research, policy, and inclusive finance.

My work combines applied economics, field research, statistical modelling, and policy advisory to support better decisions in development finance, poverty reduction, and digital transformation.

I am an economist and policy research consultant with a Ph.D. in Development Economics from the Gokhale Institute of Politics and Economics. My work focuses on financial inclusion, digital finance, women’s economic empowerment, MSME resilience, poverty, climate vulnerability, and public policy.

I have worked with institutions including Women’s World Banking, the World Bank, Asian Development Bank Institute, Indian School of Business, Government of Jharkhand, and Gokhale Institute. My experience spans cross-country research, donor-funded programmes, large-scale surveys, policy briefs, technical notes, impact-oriented studies, and stakeholder consultations.

I specialize in converting complex data and field evidence into clear insights for clients, policymakers, donors, and financial service providers. My approach combines quantitative rigour with practical understanding of implementation, institutions, and user behaviour.
